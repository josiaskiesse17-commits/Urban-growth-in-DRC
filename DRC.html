Rapid urban expansion leads to habitat loss, flooding risk, and reduced forest cover around cities , these images shows the expansion of beni city which is just next to the virunga national park, the expension of the city represent a real threat to the park and all the animals living there since the city growth means more industries , more cars , more artificial light all the time , day and night. in short more pollution and the city expansion may reach the bounderies of the park causing deforestion and loss of habitat for different animals which could lead to species extinction.

üî• Charcoal Production

Charcoal is a primary fuel source but a major driver of deforestation. Sustainable energy alternatives are essential. DRC cities are highly dependent of fossile ressources in this case charcoal which is mainly issued for preparing food . unfortunately hundreds of trees are been cut down to meet the needs of the cities , like kinshasa the capital has over 17 000 000 citizens and ranked among the fastest growing cities in terms of citizens and will likely reach 60 000 000 citizens by 2100 if nothing is done to find more ecological ressources all the forest around kinshsa and even kongo central will be cleared

Source: FAO, Global Forest Watch link
